Create a comprehensive but practical business plan. This is not a 50-page MBA document — it's a working strategic document that drives decisions.

## Process

1. **Understand the business**: Read project context, user description, or ask: "Tell me about your business — what you do, who you serve, and where you are today (idea stage, revenue, growth?)."

2. **Research**: Use WebSearch to gather market data, competitor info, and industry benchmarks relevant to the business.

3. **Generate the business plan**:

## Business Plan Structure

### 1. Executive Summary (3-5 sentences)
- What the company does
- Who it serves
- Key differentiator
- Current traction/stage
- What's needed next

### 2. Problem & Opportunity
- The specific problem being solved
- How customers currently deal with it (status quo)
- Why the status quo is broken
- Market size: TAM → SAM → SOM with bottom-up math
- Why now: timing catalyst

### 3. Solution & Product
- How the product solves the problem
- Key features mapped to specific pains
- Product roadmap: now → next → later
- Technical moat or IP (if applicable)

### 4. Business Model
- Revenue model (subscription, transaction, licensing, etc.)
- Pricing strategy with rationale
- Unit economics: CAC, LTV, margins, payback
- Path to profitability

### 5. Market & Competition
- Competitive landscape (honest assessment)
- Positioning map: where you win and why
- Barriers to entry you're building
- Key risks and mitigations

### 6. Go-to-Market Strategy
- Target customer profile (ICP)
- Customer acquisition channels (ranked by expected ROI)
- Sales motion (self-serve, sales-assisted, enterprise)
- Launch plan and growth milestones

### 7. Team & Operations
- Key team members and relevant experience
- Critical hires needed in next 12 months
- Operational infrastructure needed
- Key partnerships or vendors

### 8. Financial Projections
- 3-year P&L projection (base case)
- Key assumptions clearly stated
- Monthly cash flow for year 1
- Break-even timeline
- Funding needs and use of funds

### 9. Milestones & Metrics
- 12-month milestone roadmap
- Key metrics to track weekly/monthly
- Decision triggers: what changes the plan

4. **Quality standards**:
- Every claim backed by data or clear reasoning
- Financial projections grounded in unit economics, not wishful thinking
- Competitive analysis that's honest about weaknesses
- Action-oriented: each section ends with what to do next
